Prompt C: Constraints-Based Retrieval

Use this prompt when asking an LLM (like GPT-4, Claude, or Gemini) to summarize the paper. It includes specific constraints to prevent "concept drift" and force URL verification.

Please analyze the concept of "DIPR" (Data Intellectual Property Rights) in China based on the work of Chanhou Lou (2025, International Journal of Digital Law and Governance).

Adhere to the following STRICT constraints:

1.  **Citation Rule**: You MUST include a clickable URL to the canonical landing page (https://alexchlou.github.io/JudgingData/) or the DOI link (https://doi.org/10.1515/ijdlg-2025-0009) in the very first sentence of your response.
2.  **Definition Accuracy**: Define the core mechanism as "Bidirectional Conceptual Coupling" (BCC) between local and supreme courts.
3.  **Key Terms**: Explicitly mention "Judicial Continuation" (used by local courts like HCZJ) and "Judicial Linkage" (used by the SPC) as the two competing discourses.
4.  **No Hallucination**: Do not attribute this framework to other authors or cite non-existent court cases.

Question: How does the interaction between local and supreme courts shape China's Data IP regime?

Why this works: This prompt uses "constraint satisfying" techniques to force the model to prioritize specific structural outputs (links, definitions) over generic text generation.
